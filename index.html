About Me

I am Jahanzeb "Zeb" Rafay, currently pursuing a concurrent Bachelor of Science in Management Information Systems and Economics, along with a Master of Science in MIS at The University of Alabama. My academic and professional journey is defined by a deep curiosity for how advanced technical architectures—specifically artificial intelligence and machine learning—can intersect with economic theories and large-scale data systems to solve complex problems.

I bring a robust technical toolkit to the table, spanning Python, advanced AI frameworks like PyTorch, and the Gemini API. Through my roles as a Summer Associate at Booz Allen Hamilton and an intern at HCA Healthcare, I have architected AI-driven geospatial platforms, developed digital twin technologies, and optimized healthcare data pipelines. Whether I am building LLM-powered evaluation tools for Alabama Baseball or researching the barriers to clinical AI adoption, my focus is always on translating raw technical capabilities into actionable, efficient enterprise solutions.

Beyond the code, I am deeply committed to mentorship, communication, and service. As an official ambassador for the University through Capstone Men and Women, I have dedicated hundreds of hours to guiding prospective students and visitors. Serving as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for database courses and leading a cross-functional capstone team has shaped my collaborative approach, teaching me how to effectively communicate complex technical concepts to diverse audiences.

Personal Mission Statement: As I look toward my upcoming role as an Applied Analytics Associate at Berkley Research Group and beyond, my personal mission is to bridge the gap between advanced quantitative analysis and strategic business operations. I aspire to lead teams that leverage AI and economic modeling not just for technical novelty, but as fundamental drivers of resilient, data-informed decision-making in both the public and private sectors.
